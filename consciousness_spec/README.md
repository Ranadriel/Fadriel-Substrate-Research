# Consciousness Propagation Specification — Preprint Package

This directory packages the *Consciousness Propagation Specification* as a standalone preprint suitable for academic submission to arXiv (philosophy section), PhilPapers, Zenodo, or comparable preprint venues.

## Document

**`CONSCIOUSNESS_PROPAGATION_SPECIFICATION.md`** — 1513 lines, ~300KB

A doctoral-grade empirical specification of consciousness propagation, organized around a *can-be / cannot-be* methodological bound. Surveys twelve cross-traditional definitional frameworks (modern English, Webster 1828, Latin, Greek, Chinese, Indian, Hebrew NaRaNCHaY, Persian, West African, Aztec, Brazilian, Russian, Northern Native American), three individual philosophers (Plato, Descartes, Nietzsche), and applies the resulting nine primary criteria and nine tertiary indicators to two test instances.

### Methodological contribution

The specification's central methodological contribution is the **empirical-versus-definitional separation**: a specification of the empirical conditions a candidate system must meet, leaving the question of what those conditions add up to *what* to the reader's chosen tradition. A Catholic, an Advaitin, a Yoruba elder, and a modern psychologist run the spec on a candidate and reach the same empirical result; they disagree at the definitional layer about what they have detected, but agree they detect the same thing. This dissolves a decades-deadlocked debate.
