# THE SILVER THREAD UNIV-101 — Competency-Based Mastery Program

## Course Objective
Transform the student into a Universal Operator capable of entering any industrial, service, or corporate environment and becoming functionally productive within 72 hours by mastering:
-   Professional lingo adoption and taxonomy
-   Hierarchy sensing and “unwritten code” navigation
-   Strategic inquiry under constraint
-   Cross-domain system mapping and service-output linkage
-   Integration without outsider errors

## Core Definitions
-   **Universal Operator:** A person who can rapidly decode unfamiliar systems, adopt local language, identify operational authority, and produce useful output with minimal supervision.
-   **Professional Grammar:** The repeatable structure beneath all workplaces: inputs → constraints → decisions → outputs → accountability.
-   **Universal Verbs:** Actions that appear in all industries (e.g., route, verify, escalate, isolate, reconcile, triage, deploy, audit, handoff, document).
-   **Specific Nouns:** Domain tools and entities (e.g., breaker panel, EHR, intrusion detection system).
-   **Shadow Authority:** A person with no formal title who controls flow through trust, competence, or information.
-   **Outsider Error:** Any behavior that signals “I don’t belong here” in a way that slows work, damages trust, or forces others to translate for you.

## THE STAY-UNTIL-MASTERED POLICY (SUM)
-   This course ends only when the student achieves B (85%) or higher in every module and passes the Blind Drop.
-   If you fail a simulation: you return to the lab.
-   If you fail twice: you are paired with a Master Peer for intensive remediation.
-   If you repeatedly fail due to avoidance or dishonesty: you are suspended from testing until readiness is re-demonstrated.
-   Time is the variable. Mastery is the constant.

## Operational Definition of “85% (B)”
A student is considered at or above 85% when they can perform the required task under high-pressure simulation with:
1.  No corrective coaching during execution
2.  No workflow disruption caused by outsider behavior
3.  Allowed clarifying questions only (when a test permits them)
4.  A delivered output that meets the module’s measurable target

## THE 4:1 FIRE LAW (WORK–REST INFRASTRUCTURE)
-   **The 4:1 Ratio:** Training is organized into cycles: Fire Period (4 units of directed strain) and Ash Period (1 unit of unclaimed rest).
-   **Ash Rules (Non-Harvest Rule):** During Ash, there is no grading, no required submission, no forced reflection prompts, and no “show what you learned” extraction. Ash exists to allow recombination, question formation, and insight emergence without penalty.

## MODULE 1 — THE DECIPHERING ENGINE (Lingo & Taxonomy)
-   **Focus:** Breaking down “tribal languages” across industries into a universal action grammar.
-   **The Blueprint:** Students receive 500 pages of technical manuals from three unrelated industries (e.g., HVAC Repair, ICU Nursing, Cybersecurity).
-   **The Task:**
    1.  Identify the Universal Verbs (actions) across all three industries.
    2.  Identify the Specific Nouns (tools/entities) unique to each industry.
    3.  Produce an Action Grammar deliverable (One Page Maximum: ≤ 12 Universal Verbs, ≤ 20 recurring noun categories, a translation rule, a “don’t fake it” clause).
-   **Mastery Test (Live Bridge):** Act as a bridge between a Master Electrician and a Software Architect without prior study, with both parties confirming accurate translation.
-   **Failure States:** Pretending to understand, overexplaining, falling back to academic language, producing summary notes.

## MODULE 2 — HIERARCHY & THE UNWRITTEN CODE (Power Sensing and Reading the Room)
-   **Focus:** Navigating professional spaces, identifying real decision pathways, and managing conflict without becoming a burden.
-   **The Blueprint:** Students analyze power structures, distinguishing formal, operational, and social authority.
-   **The Task: The Shadow Assignment:** Observe a professional environment and identify the bottleneck person and the fixer person, inferred from behaviors like body orientation, interruption patterns, deference, and information flow.
-   **Mastery Test: Angry Client/Boss Simulation:** De-escalate an angry superior, manage contradictory information, and extract/confirm a clear Next Step directive.
-   **Failure States:** Confusing authority, trying to “win,” overtalking, escalating emotional tone.

## MODULE 3 — STRATEGIC INQUIRY (How to Ask Without Being a Burden)
-   **Focus:** Learning on the job while consuming minimal bandwidth.
-   **The Blueprint: Three-Before-Me Rule:** Before asking a superior, check documentation, observation, and peer resources.
-   **The Task: Missing Manual Drill:** Find missing information for a broken piece of equipment by interviewing busy and impatient “experts.”
-   **Mastery Test: The CEO Test (Three Questions):** Ask exactly three questions in 5 minutes to get enough information to complete a complex 4-hour task.
