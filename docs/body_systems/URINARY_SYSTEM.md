# The Urinary System — PhD Documentation

## Functional Overview

The urinary system filters blood, removes metabolic waste, regulates blood volume and pressure, maintains electrolyte balance, and controls acid-base homeostasis (pH 7.35-7.45). It consists of the **kidneys** (filtration), **ureters** (transport), **bladder** (storage), and **urethra** (excretion).

The kidneys are also endocrine organs producing erythropoietin (red blood cell production), renin (blood pressure via RAAS cascade), and activating vitamin D (calcium absorption for bone growth — critical at 24 months).

## Inter-Organ Equations

### Filtration Rate (GFR)

```
GFR = Filtration_Pressure × Filtration_Coefficient

In a 24-month-old:
  GFR ≈ 30-50 mL/min (compared to adult ~125 mL/min)
  Total blood filtered daily: ~50 liters
  Urine output: ~500 mL/day

Filtration Pressure = Glomerular HP - (Bowman's HP + Oncotic Pressure)
                    = 55 mmHg - (15 mmHg + 30 mmHg) = 10 mmHg net
```

### RAAS Blood Pressure Cascade

```
Low blood pressure detected by juxtaglomerular cells
  → Renin released (kidney)
  → Angiotensinogen (liver) → Angiotensin I
  → ACE (lungs) converts → Angiotensin II
  → Vasoconstriction → Blood pressure increases
  → Aldosterone released (adrenal cortex)
  → Sodium + water reabsorbed → Blood volume increases
  → Blood pressure restored

Four organs participate: Kidney → Liver → Lungs → Adrenal glands
This is a four-organ endocrine cascade crossing three organ systems.
```

### Kidney-Endocrine Connections

| Kidney Product | Target Organ | Effect |
|---------------|-------------|--------|
| Renin | Liver, lungs, adrenals | Blood pressure regulation |
| Erythropoietin | Bone marrow | Stimulates red blood cell production |
| 1,25-dihydroxyvitamin D | Intestine, bone | Calcium absorption for bone growth |
| Prostaglandins | Local kidney circulation | Vasodilation, opposes angiotensin II |
