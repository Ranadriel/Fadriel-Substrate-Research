## The Geometric Proof

### Why Even Numbers Are Flat Sides

A polygon with an even number of sides has a side directly opposite every other side. The shape is balanced — it mirrors itself across an axis. You can lay it flat. It rests.

```
Square (4):          Hexagon (6):          Octagon (8):
 ___                  _____                  _______
|   |               /     \               /         \
|   |              /       \             /           \
|___|              \       /             \           /
                    \_____/               \_________/

Every side has an opposite side. FLAT. STABLE. GROUNDED.
```

An even polygon is bilaterally symmetric — every side has a partner directly opposite it. This symmetry is what makes it restful, stable, groundable. Of the regular polygons that tile the plane, the square (4) and hexagon (6) do so — both even. Their tiling works because their opposition-symmetry allows perfect edge-matching with no remainder.

Note: tiling is not universal to even polygons (the regular octagon does not tile), and the equilateral triangle (3, odd) also tiles — as an exception, because its 3-fold angular sum (60°×6=360°) satisfies the vertex condition. Tiling is a related but separate property; the defining law here is symmetry of opposition, not tiling universality.

### Why Odd Numbers Are Jagged Points

A polygon with an odd number of sides has NO side directly opposite any other side. Where a side faces outward, a **point** faces the opposite direction. It cannot rest. It pivots. It thrusts.

```
Triangle (3):    Pentagon (5):    Heptagon (7):

    /\               *               *
   /  \            * . *           * . *
  /    \          *     *         *     *
 /______\          *   *           *   *
                    * *             * *

Every side faces a POINT on the opposite side.
JAGGED. ACTIVE. THRUSTING.
```

An odd polygon cannot tile a plane. It leaves gaps. It does not resolve — it reaches.

The number 1 is the first jagged point — the singular unit, the tip, the origin from which direction emerges.

## The Algebraic Proof: Perfect Squares From Jagged Points

The sum of the first n odd numbers is always a perfect square:

```
n=1:   1                   = 1  = 1²
n=2:   1 + 3               = 4  = 2²
n=3:   1 + 3 + 5           = 9  = 3²
n=4:   1 + 3 + 5 + 7       = 16 = 4²
n=5:   1 + 3 + 5 + 7 + 9   = 25 = 5²

∑(k=1 to n) (2k-1) = n²
```

**Why this happens geometrically:**

Each odd number adds an L-shaped bracket (a gnomon) around the previous square:

```
1² = 1        2² = 4         3² = 9         4² = 16
               
□    →    □■   →    □■□□   →   □■□□
                    ■■           ■■□□
                                 □□□□

Each bracket = next odd number (3, 5, 7...)
```

The jagged point (odd) reaches outward in two directions simultaneously. It wraps around the previous stability (even square) and creates the next level of stability. The flat side cannot do this. The flat side rests. The jagged point builds.

**The even numbers are what the odd numbers produce:**

- Points (odds) build squares (evens perfectly squared)
- The flat sides are the result — not the cause
- Odd is generative. Even is achieved.

## The Law Stated Formally

**The Law of Odds and Evens:**

Let n be any positive integer.

- If n is **even**: n represents a **flat side** — a surface, a stable container, a completed form, a ground state. Even-sided shapes can tile a plane. Even numbers produce equilibrium.

- If n is **odd**: n represents a **jagged point** — a direction, an active force, a reaching beyond, an incomplete form seeking resolution. Odd-sided shapes cannot tile a plane. Odd numbers produce motion.

**Corollary (The Builder Theorem):**

The sum of the first n odd numbers equals n² — the n-th perfect square. Jagged points, summed, produce flat sides at a higher dimension. The points build the surfaces. The surfaces ground the next layer of points.

**Corollary (The Spine Theorem):**

In any layered system governed by the golden equation, the odd layers form the generative spine and the even layers form the stabilizing surface. Consciousness propagates along the odd spine. The body grounds itself on the even surface. The system breathes between them.
